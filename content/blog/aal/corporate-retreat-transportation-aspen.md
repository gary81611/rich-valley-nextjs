Aspen hosts board meetings, founder summits, and incentive trips where the experience is the message. Ground transportation is rarely the headline — until a CEO waits twenty minutes in a slushy turnaround or two hotels worth of attendees miss a joint dinner because nobody synchronized shuttles.

Aspen Alpenglow Limousine has moved executive groups since 2012. This is the practical checklist we run with planners before the first flight lands.

## Airport reality: ASE, EGE, and DEN

Aspen/Pitkin County (ASE) is brilliantly close to town — and genuinely weather-sensitive. Eagle County (EGE) buys schedule flexibility for many national hubs. Denver International (DEN) is the reliable fallback when mountain airports wobble.

Corporate programs should document a primary airport and a fallback communication tree. We monitor flights on active transfers and adjust pickup times without spamming your VIPs. For DEN arrivals after long hauls, consider bottled water, quiet cabin expectations, and a driver who knows when to speak and when to let a passenger finish a call.

## Multi-property Aspen & Snowmass

It is common to split rooms across The Little Nell, Hotel Jerome, Limelight, and Snowmass base properties. That fragmentation is fine for lodging contracts — painful for ad hoc rideshare.

We build shuttle sheets: pickup windows, vehicle assignment, primary contact on site, and escalation numbers for the transportation captain. Sprinter vans become rolling conference rooms between venues; Suburbans handle smaller partner groups who need discretion.

## Schedule padding that still feels executive

Aspen’s roads are not urban grids. Brush Creek, Owl Creek, and canyon approaches punish optimistic Google Maps estimates in fresh snow. We pad executive transfers without embarrassing early arrivals — the goal is on-time calm, not frantic rushing.

For evening events at private ranches or mountain venues, we confirm load zones with property managers in advance. Nothing erodes a premium experience faster than a caravan of vehicles searching for a gate code in the dark.

## Billing, NDAs, and duty of care

Corporate accounts often need net terms, consolidated invoicing, and occasionally an NDA acknowledgment for high-profile passengers. We handle those administratively up front so drivers stay focused on service, not paperwork at the curb.

Duty of care also means vehicle standards: professionally detailed interiors, functioning seat belts for every passenger, and mountain tires appropriate to season. If your risk team asks for proof of insurance and operating authority, we provide it in the pre-event packet.

## When to book hourly charter vs point-to-point

Point-to-point works for airport legs and single dinner transfers. Hourly charter wins when agendas breathe: a morning off-site, an afternoon studio visit, and a loose-ended evening. You pay for readiness — a staged vehicle when the meeting runs long is cheaper than reconstructing last-minute rides from zero.
